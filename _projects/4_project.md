This project investigates the first-in-human technical feasibility of intraoperative photoacoustic and ultrasound (PA/US) imaging for human brain tumor surgery. Using a handheld LED-based PA/US system and low-dose indocyanine green (ICG), we demonstrated depth-resolved visualization of endogenous vasculature and ICG-enhanced contrast during surgery. The study included imaging at the cortical surface, in excised tumor specimens, and within the surgical cavity, highlighting the potential of PA/US as a clinically translatable adjunct for neurosurgical guidance.

This work combines endogenous photoacoustic vascular imaging at 850 nm with differential ICG-enhanced imaging at 820/940 nm. By integrating photoacoustic molecular contrast with co-registered ultrasound structure, the system provides complementary information beyond surface-limited fluorescence imaging and conventional intraoperative ultrasound.

Overall, this study shows that intraoperative PA/US imaging can provide depth-resolved structural and molecular information in human brain tumor surgery while remaining compatible with standard surgical workflow. This approach may support future development of clinically practical imaging tools for improved tumor visualization and surgical guidance.
